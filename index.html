OddJob


❮ ❯

OddJob is a sharing economy website which lets users create work for one another and engage in transactions.
It is designed for a quick and convenient place to post or search for a job in your local area.

Before completing a job or before sending payment, each user must rate each other on their interaction.
The higher your rating, the more jobs or job offers you will likely receive.

OddJob was developed by myself and James Ross using HTML, JavaScript, PHP, and MySQL for our 400-level
social computing course.

My main contribution was the front-end design and user interaction.
We practiced pair programming for the back-end work and the setup of our databases.

OG: Original Gladiator


Original Gladiator is a simple RPG inspired by the flash game Swords and Sandals.
It is designed as a quick and easy game to pick up and play without a steep learning curve.

Users can customize the look of their players, upgrade their stats, and armour to fight progressivly stronger enemies in the arena.
The longer you last, the more you get paid.

Original Gladiator was created by five developers in Unity as a term project for our 300-level game mechanics class.
My main contribution was the design, development, and testing of the enemy AI.
I also assited in some of the artwork and UI, as well as the audio and visual effects.
